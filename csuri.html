Charles Csuri — A Lineage
← Back to timeline
Charles Csuri has surfaced in the archive four times across four months — each time from a different angle: a figurative source dissolved into algorithmic accumulation, the underdocumented process behind it, his place in a wider lineage of machine-made abstraction, and finally his own 1967 prophecy of computer-generated sculpture, fulfilled decades later. Same artist, four distinct readings.

The Throughline

Csuri began each era as a human figure or process, hand-traced or hand-described, then let the machine's own logic take over — a drum plotter's dense contour lines, a coordinate digitizer's discrete samples, a wave-interference pattern standing in for pen-drawn sine curves. Days 58–59 read him through his process; Day 72 places him in company; Day 79 lets him speak for himself. Four renders, four palettes, four mechanical characters — one throughline: the human origin never fully disappears, it just changes how visibly it survives the machine.
