What this is

A generative art engine that creates one artwork per day, shaped entirely by @Noahbolanowski's tweets about art and technology history. Each piece encodes the themes, images, and emotional register of a day's worth of curatorial thought — then breeds with its ancestors to evolve over time.

How it works

Tweets are scraped nightly and analyzed for visual and thematic signals — palettes, motifs, dominant concepts. A genome of ~25 parameters is biased toward the day's content. 12 candidates render; the best is selected by an aesthetic fitness function (with occasional curatorial overrides when theme and fitness disagree). The winning genome enters an ancestry archive and breeds future generations.

The archive

Every card is a day. The condensed reason describes what the system heard in the tweets and why it made the choices it made — an algorithmic catalogue note. Dashed lines between cards show lineage: when a winner's genome influenced a later generation. ★ marks Hall of Fame entries that spawned three or more descendants.
